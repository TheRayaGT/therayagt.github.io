I believe every Mathematician has his/her favourite formula. I do have one as well and of course, it belongs to the realm of Number Theory. That is

\( p(n) \sim \frac{1}{4n\sqrt{3}}\operatorname{exp}\left(\pi\sqrt{\frac{2n}{3}}\right) \) for large \(n\).

This formula for the number of partitions of an integer \(p(n)\) was developed by Hardy and Ramanujan. In my opinion, it is so beautiful since it reminds me of the Most Beautiful Mathematical Equation coined by Euler, which is \(e^{i\pi}+1=0\). The asymptotic formula for the number of partitions of partitions encompasses both \(\pi\) and \(e\), which are two of the most (actually 'the two most') famous constants in Mathematics. Moreover, partitions are very closely-tied to the distribution of identical objects into identical bins, which is an interesting problem in Combinatorics.
